The beginning of crypto

Bitcoin emerged out of the 2008 financial crisis as an alternative monetary instrument, not controlled by any central authority or government. The success of btc paved the way for others to experiment creating new cryptocurrencies. But this diverted money away from btc and over time ate away at its market share.

Heart's Law

Cryptocurrencies are highly correlated with each - in other words, they all move together. A key reason for this is explained with Heart's Law: price movements of interchangeable assets are connected through the liquidity of their trading pairs.[1]

Standard deviation

Sd is a measure of volatility, and in financial markets its one measure of risk. The more the asset deviates from its mean (higher Sd), the riskier it is. Sd will later be used for risk-adjmentment calculations.[2]
